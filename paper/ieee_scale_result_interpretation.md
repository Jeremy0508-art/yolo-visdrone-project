## Evidence-Bounded Interpretation

- Compared with YOLO11n-960, YOLO11n-P2-960 improves small-object recall by `0.029865` (2.99 percentage points).
- YOLO11n-P2-CA-960 improves small-object recall by `0.034830` (3.48 percentage points) over YOLO11n-960.
- YOLO11s-960 remains the strongest small-object recall reference, with a `0.072444` (7.24 percentage points) recall gain over YOLO11n-960.
- P2/CA improves small-scale recall on VisDrone validation, but the medium/large-scale rows show trade-offs rather than uniform gains across all scales.
- The correct wording is scale-wise recall/precision improvement, not AP-small improvement.
- These results support the lightweight trade-off narrative, not a claim that the nano model outperforms larger-capacity detectors.

## Manuscript Use

Allowed wording:

> On the VisDrone validation split, scale-wise analysis shows that the P2 high-resolution branch increases small-object recall compared with the YOLO11n-960 baseline, while YOLO11s-960 remains stronger in absolute recall. This suggests that high-resolution shallow prediction helps lightweight models recover small objects, but larger capacity still provides a higher upper reference.

Avoid wording:

- `The method improves AP-small` unless AP-specific evaluation is added.
- `The lightweight model is best across all scales`.
- `The method outperforms YOLO11s`.
- `The result proves cross-dataset generalization`.
